Food Chains vs. Food Webs

A Food Chain follows a single path as animals eat each other (e.g., Grass → Rabbit → Fox). A Food Web shows how multiple food chains overlap, representing the true complexity of an Ohio forest or pond.

The Trophic Levels:

  • Producers (Autotrophs): Level 1. Capture solar energy (e.g., Algae, Corn).
  • Primary Consumers: Level 2. Herbivores that eat producers (e.g., Grasshopper).
  • Secondary Consumers: Level 3. Carnivores eating herbivores (e.g., Bluegill Fish).
  • Tertiary/Quaternary: Levels 4+. Apex predators (e.g., Osprey).

The 10% Rule & Biomass

In ecology, a Pyramid of Energy is always upright. Why? Because only 10% of energy is transferred. The rest is lost as heat!

Biomass: The total mass of all organisms in a trophic level. Like energy, biomass typically decreases as you move up the pyramid.
