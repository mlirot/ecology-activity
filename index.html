Section 1: Who Eats Whom?

Energy begins with the Sun. In Ohio, Producers (like White Oak trees or Switchgrass) capture this energy via photosynthesis.

  • Primary Consumers: Herbivores (e.g., Eastern Cottontail Rabbit)
  • Secondary Consumers: Carnivores that eat herbivores (e.g., Garter Snake)
  • Tertiary Consumers: Eat secondary consumers (e.g., Red-tailed Hawk)
  • Quaternary Consumers: Apex predators (e.g., Coyote)

Section 2: The 10% Rule

Only about 10% of energy is passed to the next level. The rest is lost as heat or used for metabolic processes.
